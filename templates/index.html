How SVD Compression Works

1

Decompose

Each colour channel of the image (an m × n matrix A) is factored into three matrices:

A = U · Σ · Vᵀ

U (m × m) holds left singular vectors, Σ (m × n) holds singular values in decreasing order, Vᵀ (n × n) holds right singular vectors.

2

Truncate

Keep only the top k singular values and their corresponding vectors. This forms a rank-k approximation:

Ak = Uk · Σk · Vkᵀ

The Eckart-Young theorem guarantees this is the best rank-k approximation in Frobenius norm.

3

Reconstruct

The compressed image is rebuilt from fewer numbers: instead of storing m × n values, you store k × (m + n + 1).

CR = (m · n) / (k · (m + n + 1))

Smaller k = more compression but lower quality. The trick is finding the sweet spot.

4

Evaluate

Quality is measured via multiple metrics:

  • PSNR — higher is better (>30 dB is good)
  • SSIM — closer to 1 is better
  • MSE — lower is better
  • CR — higher means more compression
